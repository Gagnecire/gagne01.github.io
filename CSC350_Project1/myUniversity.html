Borough of Manhattan Community College

Borough of Manhattan Community College , commonly known as BMCC, is located at 199 Chambers Street, in the lively Tribeca neighborhood of New York City. BMCC was founded in 1963 and opened in 1964 as a small, primarily business-oriented community college offering programs aimed at the business community.
Originally located in two floors of a commercial building in midtown Manhattan, the college focused on preparing students for business careers and on providing a liberal arts education to students who wished to transfer to four-year colleges. The college also created on-site training and management development courses for mid-level employees. BMCC continuously modified its in-house and on-site programs.

Activities at BMCC

Student clubs and organizations are a fun and vital part of the BMCC experience. They are chartered to serve the diverse needs and interests of our students. They are avenues for forming friendships, cultural expression, skill development or aiding you in your future career.

Soccer Club

A college soccer club is a student-led group focused on promoting soccer within the campus community. It provides a space for students of all skill levels to play and enjoy the sport together. The club organizes practices, matches, and may participate in intercollegiate competitions.

Co-Curricular Transcript

A co-curricular transcript is a document that highlights your non-academic activities and achievements in school or college. It complements your academic transcript and showcases your holistic development. It's useful for demonstrating your well-roundedness to employers or further educational institutions.

Sculpture Club

A college sculpture club is a student-led group dedicated to the creation and appreciation of three-dimensional art forms. Members collaborate on projects, exchange ideas, and host exhibitions to showcase their sculpting talents in a creative and supportive environment.
